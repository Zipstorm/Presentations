Product context

SeekOut.ai (codename: Helix) — a consumer-facing hiring platform connecting three personas through sharing-driven viral loops.

Candidates

Upload → diagnose → build → share

"Upload your resume. Get an AI diagnosis in 60 seconds. Walk away with a portfolio that actually gets noticed."

Hiring managers

Post → share → screen → review

"Post a job. Get AI-screened candidates with structured feedback tools."

Recruiters

Intake → screen → packet → feedback

"AI intake in 5 minutes. Screening at scale. Built by SeekOut."

Key product mechanics

  • Every portfolio share, job link share, and prospect packet is a tracked link that pulls new users into the platform
  • Candidates get standalone value on day one (AI resume diagnosis + portfolio builder) — no network required
  • HMs and candidates create content (jobs, portfolios) that naturally distributes through sharing
  • One user can be multiple personas (e.g. an HM who also builds a portfolio)

Strategic constraints

Why we're phasing carefully and what's ready at launch.

SeekOut has 1,000+ enterprise customers — warm relationships with thousands of HMs and recruiters. We only get one shot with that network. If we invite them before the product delivers clear value, we burn trust and lose our strongest distribution channel.

The enterprise network

  • Not off-limits — but no official outreach until we have organic traction and validated value
  • Enterprise activation is Phase 4 (separate plan)
  • When we reach out, it should feel like "come see what 500 HMs are already using" — not "try our beta"

Relationship to SeekOut Recruit

SeekOut.ai is a separate consumer product. Different brand, different growth model (viral vs. sales-led), different audience (individuals vs. enterprise TA teams). But built by the same team, on the same infrastructure, with the same AI. The "built by SeekOut" trust signal is an asset — use it, especially with recruiters.

Phase 0: Prep

Product/eng driving toward internal go-live. Marketing prepares.

  • Build recruiter waitlist page and mechanics
  • Seed content pipeline: 5+ pieces ready to publish at public launch
  • Start SEO content (long lead time — compounds later)
  • Open question Should SeekOut.ai have its own LinkedIn company page?
  • Prepare social content calendar
  • Set up analytics dashboards

Phase 1: Internal dogfood

~140 SeekOut employees use the live product. Marketing's role is minimal.

  • Observe real usage patterns — what do people share? What language do they use?
  • Capture screenshots, quotes, before/after examples (with permission)
  • Refine messaging based on what resonates internally
  • Continue building public launch assets
  • Do not activate any external channels yet

Exit criteria: Qualitative — internal feedback is positive enough to go public (PM decides).

Phase 2: Public launch

Open signup for candidates and HMs. Recruiter waitlist goes live. This is the phase where marketing proves its value.

  • Activate all organic channels (LinkedIn, Reddit, SEO)
  • Drive candidate signups through content marketing
  • Drive HM signups through LinkedIn thought leadership + selective direct outreach
  • Build and grow the recruiter waitlist
  • Test messaging/positioning with small paid ad spend

Phase 3: Momentum plays

Organic traction validated. Time to amplify.

  • Coordinated launch moment: Product Hunt, Hacker News, or equivalent
  • Activate influencer partnerships (career coaches, recruiting creators)
  • Begin university/bootcamp partnerships
  • Scale what's working from Phase 2, cut what isn't

Trigger: PM and marketing jointly decide based on Phase 2 growth trajectory, retention, and loop health.

Phase 4: Enterprise activation

Activate SeekOut's 500+ enterprise customer network. This phase gets its own plan.

  • CS/AE teams drive outreach (warm intros to HMs and recruiters at existing customers)
  • Recruiter waitlist may be partially or fully opened
  • Marketing provides assets, landing pages, email templates, success stories
  • This is the highest-leverage growth moment — we need to be ready

Paid ads: messaging testing only

Budget goal is learning, not volume. Keep spend at ~$500–1K/week max during testing. Kill anything that isn't generating learning.
  • A/B test headlines, value props, and positioning across personas
  • Identify which messages drive clicks vs. actual signups
  • Test candidate vs. HM messaging independently
  • Build a library of validated messaging for organic channels

Persona playbooks

Tactics by persona, in priority order.

Candidates — volume play

Open signup

The hook: "Upload your resume. Get an AI diagnosis in 60 seconds. Walk away with a portfolio that actually gets noticed."

The magic moment: The before/after of AI resume diagnosis.

Tactics

  1. "Resume before/after" content — core content engine
  2. SEO targeting "AI resume builder," "resume tips 2026"
  3. Reddit presence — authentic, no shilling
  4. "Score share" mechanic — "My resume went from 62 → 91"
  5. University/bootcamp outreach (Phase 3)
Hiring managers — quality play

Open signup

The hook: "Post a job. Get AI-screened candidates with structured feedback tools. No recruiter middleman needed."

Tactics

  1. LinkedIn thought leadership — earn trust, soft CTAs
  2. Job sharing loop — beautiful Open Graph link previews
  3. Selective direct outreach at launch (outside enterprise base)
  4. Private HM community (Slack/Discord) for early adopters
Recruiters — demand building

Waitlisted

The hook: "The AI recruiting tool that actually works — built by SeekOut, the company 1,000+ enterprises already trust."

Tactics

  1. Leverage enterprise brand trust signal
  2. Waitlist counter as social proof
  3. Weekly "drop" emails — sneak peeks, demo clips
  4. "AI intake in 5 minutes" demo video
  5. Influencer fast-track (5K+ followers get early access)
